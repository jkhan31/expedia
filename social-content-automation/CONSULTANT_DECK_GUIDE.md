# Consultant Deck Structure: Golden Circle Framework

## Core Principle

The best consultant decks are built on the Golden Circle, just with more depth.

```
WHY (Why does this matter?)     → 5-7 slides
    ↓
HOW (How did we analyze it?)   → 10-15 slides
    ↓
WHAT (What should we do?)      → 5-10 slides
```

This is how McKinsey, Bain, and BCG structure decks. It's not narrative fluff—it's strategic clarity.

---

## Part 1: WHY (5-7 slides)

### Goal
Make the reader care. Establish why this matters. Create urgency.

### Slide Sequence

**Slide 1: Cover**
- Title: The recommendation or finding
- Subtitle: The business context
- Example: "Luxury Hotel Marketplace Strategy | Ranking Visibility Opportunity"

**Slide 2: Situation**
- The current state
- Market context
- Example: "Luxury hotels book 27% less than budget hotels on Expedia"

**Slide 3: The Problem**
- Why it matters
- Business impact
- Example: "This represents $50M+ in unrealized marketplace revenue"

**Slide 4: The Assumption**
- What people think is causing it
- The conventional wisdom
- Example: "Most operators assume this is a price/demand problem"

**Slide 5: The Insight**
- What the data actually shows
- The surprise/paradox
- Example: "But the root cause is visibility, not demand"

**Slide 6: The Opportunity**
- What fixing this enables
- The upside
- Example: "Rebalancing rankings for segment quality could unlock $50M+"

**Slide 7: How We'll Analyze It**
- Brief overview of approach
- Preview of what's coming
- Example: "We analyzed 100K+ searches to understand ranking behavior"

### Design Principle
**Each slide should answer one question the reader is asking.**

Not: "Here's data about luxury hotels"
But: "Why should I care about luxury hotel bookings?"

---

## Part 2: HOW (10-15 slides)

### Goal
Prove your findings are credible. Show your methodology. Build trust through evidence.

### Slide Sequence

**Slide 8: Methodology Overview**
- How you approached the analysis
- Data sources
- Framework
- Example: "Analysis of 100K+ searches, segmented by booking rates and search frequency"

**Slide 9: Data Collection**
- What data you used
- Sample size
- Time period
- Example: "Expedia search data, 2024 Q1-Q2, all hotel searches, US market"

**Slide 10: Segment Analysis**
- Break down the finding by segment
- Show the gap clearly
- Example: Budget vs. Luxury booking rates side by side

**Slide 11: The Booking Rate Gap**
- Visualize the finding
- Make it undeniable
- Example: Chart showing luxury 2.20% vs. budget 3.04%

**Slide 12: What Drives Ranking?**
- Explain the mechanism
- How algorithms work
- Example: "Marketplace ranking optimizes for conversion, not segment balance"

**Slide 13: Why Luxury Gets Deprioritized**
- The HOW (the mechanism)
- Why this happens
- Example: "Luxury appears in fewer searches, so has lower booking probability, so gets ranked lower"

**Slide 14: Search Volume Analysis**
- Show that luxury searches are lower
- Prove visibility is the issue
- Example: "Luxury: 8% of searches, 5% of bookings | Budget: 75% of searches, 80% of bookings"

**Slide 15: Alternative Hypotheses Tested**
- What else could explain it?
- Why we ruled them out
- Example: "Tested: price sensitivity (ruled out), quality (ruled out), availability (ruled out)"

**Slide 16: The Evidence Summary**
- Recap of HOW
- One-pager of findings
- Example: "Root cause is visibility, not demand or price"

**Slide 17: Implications of This Finding**
- What this means
- Why this matters strategically
- Example: "If visibility improves, bookings should improve proportionally"

### Design Principle
**Show, don't tell. Use data to prove each step.**

Not: "We found that visibility is the problem"
But: [Chart showing visibility gap] [Search volume analysis] [Booking probability by visibility]

---

## Part 3: WHAT (5-10 slides)

### Goal
Tell them what to do. Make it actionable. Make it compelling.

### Slide Sequence

**Slide 18: The Core Recommendation**
- What should they do?
- One clear statement
- Example: "Rebalance ranking algorithm to optimize for segment quality, not just conversion"

**Slide 19: Why This Recommendation?**
- Connect back to the finding
- Show this solves the problem
- Example: "If visibility improves from 8% to 12%, luxury bookings increase by 50%+"

**Slide 20: How to Implement**
- The operational steps
- What needs to happen
- Example: "1) Adjust ranking weights | 2) A/B test | 3) Monitor segment metrics"

**Slide 21: Expected Impact**
- What will change
- Quantified upside
- Example: "$50M+ revenue opportunity, improved customer experience, better marketplace equilibrium"

**Slide 22: Risks & Mitigations**
- What could go wrong
- How to manage it
- Example: "Risk: Budget conversion drops | Mitigation: Gradual rollout, A/B test"

**Slide 23: Timeline**
- When to do this
- Phase planning
- Example: "Phase 1 (weeks 1-4): Testing | Phase 2 (weeks 5-8): Rollout"

**Slide 24: Next Steps**
- What happens after this deck
- Clear action items
- Example: "1) Approve recommendation | 2) Allocate engineering | 3) Set metrics"

**Slide 25: Closing**
- Loop back to opening
- Reinforce the opportunity
- Example: "$50M opportunity waiting. Luxury visibility is the lever. Let's pull it."

### Design Principle
**Make it so obvious they can't say no.**

Not: "We recommend this"
But: "This solves the $50M problem. Here's exactly how."

---

## Why This Structure Works for Consultant Decks

### 1. It's How Readers Think
- "Why should I care?" (WHY)
- "Is this real?" (HOW)
- "What do I do?" (WHAT)

Answer these three questions clearly, and they buy it.

### 2. It Creates Narrative Flow
Decks aren't data dumps. They're arguments.
- WHY: Establish the stakes
- HOW: Build credibility
- WHAT: Close the deal

### 3. It Respects Time
Executives skim decks.
- WHY tells them if they should read at all
- HOW tells them if it's credible
- WHAT tells them what to do

### WHAT Section (Slides 18-25)

## The Golden Circle in Consultant Decks

**Same framework. Different scale.**

| Element | Social Post | Consultant Deck |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| **WHY** | 1-2 sentences | 5-7 slides |
| **HOW** | 1-2 sentences | 10-15 slides |
| **WHAT** | 1-2 sentences | 5-10 slides |

The logic is identical. The depth is proportional to the context.

## Design Principles for Consultant Decks

### 1. One Idea Per Slide
Each slide answers one question.

Not: "Luxury hotels, booking rates, search volume, and recommendations"
But: [Slide 1: Luxury booking rates] [Slide 2: Search volume] [Slide 3: Recommendation]

### 2. Show, Don't Tell
Use visuals to carry the argument.

Not: "Luxury hotels appear in fewer searches"
But: [Chart showing luxury search share vs. budget search share]

### 3. Data > Opinion
Every claim is backed by data.

Not: "We think visibility is the problem"
But: [Evidence showing visibility gap] [Data showing causation]

### 4. Narrative Over Presentation
The deck tells a story, not a list.

Not: "Key findings: 1) X  2) Y  3) Z"
But: "Why luxury underperforms → How we discovered why → What to do about it"

### 5. Obvious Close
The recommendation should feel inevitable by the time you reach it.

---

## Golden Circle Checklist for Consultant Decks

**WHY (Does the reader care?)**
- [ ] Opening clearly states the problem
- [ ] Problem has a quantified impact
- [ ] The assumption/surprise is clear
- [ ] Opportunity is compelling
- [ ] Reader is ready to learn HOW

**HOW (Is the analysis credible?)**
- [ ] Methodology is transparent
- [ ] Data sources are solid
- [ ] Analysis is rigorous
- [ ] Alternative hypotheses are tested
- [ ] Conclusion is undeniable by the end

**WHAT (Is the action clear?)**
- [ ] Recommendation is specific
- [ ] Recommendation solves the problem
- [ ] Implementation is realistic
- [ ] Impact is quantified
- [ ] Next steps are obvious

23. Expected impact & risks
24. Timeline
25. Next steps
26. Closing

## Not Weird. Actually Ideal.

The Golden Circle works for consultant decks because strategic decks **are already built on it**.

The difference: Social content compresses it into one post. Consultant decks expand it across a presentation.

**Same thinking. Different scale. Same power.**

---

## Apply This to Your Expedia Deck

If you're presenting this analysis to stakeholders:

1. **WHY:** Lead with the surprise (not price, visibility)
2. **HOW:** Show the analysis (data, methodology, proof)
3. **WHAT:** Close with the recommendation (rebalance ranking)

The deck becomes not just analysis, but argument.
Not just findings, but strategy.
Not just "here's what we learned," but "here's what you should do."

That's consultant deck gold.
