# Content Style Guide: Golden Circle Framework

## Core Principle

Every post (regardless of platform) follows Simon Sinek's Golden Circle:

```
         WHY (Core belief/insight)
              ↓
         HOW (Mechanism/principle)
              ↓
         WHAT (Specific data/finding)
```

**Why this matters:**
- WHY engages emotion (why should I care?)
- HOW explains logic (how does this work?)
- WHAT provides proof (here's the evidence)

Without WHY, content is forgettable.
Without HOW, content lacks understanding.
Without WHAT, content lacks credibility.

All three are required. Order matters: lead with WHY.

---

## Golden Circle Template

### WHY (The Insight)
**The core belief or principle your data reveals.**

Questions to ask:
- What assumption does this challenge?
- What would be true if this finding is correct?
- Why should someone care about this pattern?

Example: "Most people get this wrong: what we assume drives behavior often doesn't match what actually happens."

### HOW (The Mechanism)
**The principle or pattern that explains the finding.**

Questions to ask:
- What principle is at work?
- How does this pattern show up?
- What's the mechanism underneath?

Example: "When you measure actual behavior (not assumptions), you discover different priorities than people think."

### WHAT (The Evidence)
**The specific, quantified finding.**

Questions to ask:
- What's the data?
- What's the specific insight?
- What can be measured/proven?

Example: "Luxury hotels book 27% less on Expedia. Price isn't why—ranking visibility is."

## Platform-Specific Execution

Each platform has different constraints and audiences. 

**But the Golden Circle structure remains constant.**

Only the emphasis and format change.

---

## LinkedIn: Professional WHY

### Structure
**WHY (Business Impact)** → HOW (Market Principle) → WHAT (Data + Opportunity)

### Hook (Punchy opening, leads with WHY)
Open with the counterintuitive insight or market opportunity.

Examples:
- "You'd think price drives luxury hotel bookings. You'd be wrong."
- "Most booking gaps aren't price problems. They're visibility problems."
- "The #1 reason luxury hotels underperform isn't what you think."

### Body (HOW)
Explain the principle: "When we measure actual behavior vs. assumptions, the pattern becomes clear..."

### Close (WHAT + CTA)
State the data and call to action: "Luxury: 2.20% booking rate vs. Budget: 3.04%. [Full analysis: link]"

### Tone
Professional, data-driven, thought leadership.

### Length
100-150 words

### Example

```
You'd think luxury hotel bookings drop because of price.

Wrong.

When you measure what actually drives bookings on Expedia, you find something different:

📊 Booking rates

The gap isn't price elasticity—it's ranking visibility. Luxury hotels are deprioritized in search results, driving searchers to alternatives.

For marketplace operators: This is a $50M+ revenue opportunity. For hotels: Ranking optimization beats price cuts.

The root cause matters more than the symptom.

Read the full analysis: [link]
```

## Instagram: Human WHY

### Structure
**WHY (Myth-Busting)** → HOW (Surprising Pattern) → WHAT (Accessible Data)

### Hook (Visual + Text)
Open with a contrarian statement or surprising reveal.

Examples:
- "Plot twist 🔄 You think luxury hotels book less because price is high. Actually..."
- "Myth: Luxury = High Price. Reality: Luxury = Low Visibility"
- "The #1 reason people book budget hotels over luxury isn't what you'd expect 👇"

### Body (HOW + WHAT)
Explain the pattern in casual, accessible language. Include the data as key insight.

### Close (Emoji + CTA)
End with engagement: "What surprised you? 👇" or "Link in bio for full analysis 🔗"

### Tone
Conversational, myth-busting, accessible.

### Length
80-120 words

### Example

```
Plot twist 🔄

You think luxury hotels book less because they're expensive.

Actually, it's the opposite.

When we analyzed 100k+ searches on Expedia:

Luxury booking rate: 2.20%
Budget booking rate: 3.04%

The gap? Ranking visibility. Luxury hotels aren't even showing up in the right positions.

It's not a price problem.
It's a visibility problem.

Full breakdown 🔗 Link in bio

#marketplace #data #insights
```

## Twitter: Punchy WHY

### Structure
**WHY (Data Paradox)** → HOW (Thread Narrative) → WHAT (Clear Conclusion)

### Hook (Tweet 1)
Open with the contrarian insight as a thread opener.

Examples:
- "Luxury hotels book 27% less on Expedia. But it's not why you think. 🧵"
- "Thread: Why everyone's wrong about why luxury hotels underperform"
- "Most people think price drives booking gaps. The data disagrees. 🧵"

### Body (Tweets 2-7)
Walk through the logic: assumption → data → discovery → implication

### Close (Tweet 8)
State the finding clearly. Include link.

### Tone
Conversational, narrative, punchy.

### Example

```

Tweet 7:
It's not a price problem—it's a ranking problem. Different fix. Bigger opportunity.

Tweet 8:

```

## Blog Post: Full Golden Circle

### Structure
**WHY (Compelling Narrative)** → HOW (Detailed Explanation) → WHAT (Data + Implications)

### Title (Intriguing, Hints at Content)
Lead with the paradox or myth-busting angle.

Examples:
- "Why Luxury Hotels Book Less (And It Has Nothing to Do With Price)"
- "The Ranking Visibility Problem That Kills Luxury Hotel Bookings"
- "What 100K Hotel Searches Revealed About Marketplace Behavior"

### Opening (Hook + WHY)
Start with the counterintuitive insight. Make the reader curious.

### Body (HOW)
Walk through the analysis process. Explain the principle. Show the methodology.

### Data Section (WHAT)
Present the findings with context. Use visualizations if possible.

### Implications (WHY + WHAT Combined)
What does this mean? Why should we care? What's the opportunity?

### Close
Return to the opening insight. Reinforce the surprise.

## How to Apply This Framework

### Step 1: Identify WHY
Before writing any post, ask:
- What assumption does this challenge?
- What principle is revealed?
- Why should my audience care?

Write this down as one sentence: "The WHY is..."

### Step 2: Explain HOW
Ask:
- What's the principle or mechanism?
- How does this pattern work?
- What process led to this discovery?

Write this down as one sentence: "The HOW is..."

### Step 3: Present WHAT
Ask:
- What's the specific data?
- What can be measured?
- How do I prove this?

Write this down as one sentence: "The WHAT is..."

### Step 4: Choose Your Platform
Different platforms emphasize different parts of the circle.

**LinkedIn:** WHY (opportunity) → WHAT (data) → HOW (execution)
**Instagram:** WHY (myth-busting) → HOW (surprising) → WHAT (data)
**Twitter:** WHY (paradox) → HOW (narrative) → WHAT (conclusion)
**Blog:** WHY → HOW → WHAT (full depth)

### Step 5: Write
Use the Golden Circle template for your platform.
Lead with the hook that emphasizes WHY.

## Key Rules

1. **Always lead with WHY** — The insight, the surprise, the reason to care
2. **Keep HOW clear** — Don't make people guess the principle
3. **Always back it with WHAT** — Data, evidence, proof
4. **Adjust platform, not framework** — Same circle, different emphasis
5. **Punchy hook first** — Every post opens with something that makes people stop and read
6. **Consistent voice** — Same core insight, different platform language

---

## Why This Works

**WHY:** Emotional connection (makes people care)
**HOW:** Logical understanding (makes it believable)
**WHAT:** Proof (makes it credible)

Content without WHY is data nobody cares about.
Content without HOW is claims nobody believes.
Content without WHAT is noise.

**Golden Circle = complete communication.**

## For Every Project

Apply this framework to every new analysis/project:

1. **Extract WHY:** What assumption does my finding challenge?
2. **Clarify HOW:** What principle explains this?
3. **Present WHAT:** What's the data?
4. **Write variations:** LinkedIn (WHY→WHAT), Instagram (WHY→HOW), Twitter (WHY thread), Blog (WHY→HOW→WHAT)
5. **Keep hooks punchy:** Open every post with the surprise, the insight, the "wait, really?"

---

**Remember:** The data is the same. The WHY is the same. Only the packaging changes per platform.

Golden Circle framework ensures every piece of content is complete, compelling, and credible.
